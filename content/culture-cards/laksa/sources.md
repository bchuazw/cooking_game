## Pitfall checks

- ✅ Variant named (Katong / Peranakan / Nyonya); does not call laksa "Singaporean curry noodles".
- ✅ Penang and Sarawak relatives explicitly acknowledged in body.
- ✅ Daun kesum identified as not-mint.
- ✅ Halal: shipped recipe uses prawns, fishcake, tau pok — no pork or non-halal animal proteins. Halal-compatible.

## Conflict resolution

Some sources call short rice noodles in laksa "thick beehoon", others "laksa
noodle". We use "thick rice noodles" and footnote the alternative.
